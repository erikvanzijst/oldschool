Object-oriented software development @ CS.VU in 2002
--- List of paper projects ---

Lecturer: Ralf Lämmel (ralf@cs.vu.nl)
Last updated on 7 March 2003.

For each paper, the strengths and weaknesses are listed. There is at least one grade, namely the grade that will apply if the paper will not be revised until the deadline. The deadline is 8 April 2003 if not negotiated otherwise. So strictly speaking, the papers do not need to be revised. For several papers, a revision is encouraged. That is, fixing some of the weaknesses, or building upon some strengths, the paper could be improved. A better grade for such a revised paper is then explicitly listed. Two forms of revision are distinguished. Firstly, a minor revision asks for maturation like covering obvious omissions in the discussions and completing bibliographic references. Secondly, a major revision asks for a substantial extension. and for substantial rewriting, which might imply some extra research. Most of the weaknesses need to be addressed for both a minor and major revision, while a major revision normally attempts a more complete job as opposed to a quick fix done in a minor revision. Some weaknesses are tagged with "desirable"; this can be taken as an indication that they are not relevant for a minor revision. Sometimes both a minor and a major revision are encouraged. Clearly, in this case the major revision must be regarded as a challenge. In general, the higher the promised grade, the more difficult it is to succeed. In submitting a revision, please state clearly why you think that your submission achieves a minor or a major revision.
