As the title says the course consists of carrying on a literature study on a topic chose in agreement with your supervisor. Select a topic of your interest or a course that you particularly like. Contact the the person in charge of the research area/course you picked and discuss with him the possibility to carry out a literature study under his supervision. Once agreed on the topic the study is articulated in three phases:
    1. Search of related bibliographic material (i.e. papers, reports, etc.)
    2. Preparation of a slide-show (using any technology and/or tool you feel more apropriate) that introduces the topic to an audience of computer scientist that are however not necessarily expert on the topic presented. Following the introduction the presentation should illustrate and explain the findings of your study.
    3. Give a conference-style talk to present the content of your study supported by the slides you just prepared. The talk will be given to an audience of CS Master students and possibly scientific staff. The presentation will last 20 minutes with an additional time of 5-10 minutes for questions.

The talk will be evaluated and graded by your supervisor.
You have to contact the coordinator to arrange the scheule of your talk or for any further information about procedural matters.

Schedule

There will be some days allocated and dedicated to the presentations. Students can book and register for a slot to give their presentation by sending a mail to the coordinator. The exact dates will be posted soon on this page. The first one will be in the second half of November (probably 25th) and the second one around the middle of December (probably 11th).
